Desktop Hardware

Based on your answers we think you should use the Crescent Purchasing Consortium (CPC) framework Desktop hardware.

What it offers

This framework is an agreement for the supply of a wide range of ICT equipment and supporting services, which runs until 17 January 2020.

Products and services include:

  • IT hardware and components (monitors, keyboards, mice, thin clients, and storage).
  • desktop PCs
  • laptops
  • tablets
  • notebooks
  • Apple products (iPads, iPhones, iMacs and accessories)
  • services for hardware installation, maintenance, and staff training
  • IT equipment disposal and recycling, for products including computers, printers, laptops, smart devices, telephony, and cabling

Benefits

The framework is quick and easy with online quotes and offers substantial savings on IT brands.

Benefits include:

  • access to a range of well-known and reputable IT brands
  • over 20 specialist ICT hardware suppliers, to help you maximise competition
  • substantial education discounts relative to the market place
  • a quick route to market through direct call-off or mini competition
  • average savings of 18.69% (as at June 2017)
